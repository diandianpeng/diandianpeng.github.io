Research Experience

  • Postdoctoral Scholar, Scripps Institution of Oceanography, UC San Diego, June 2022 – June 2025

    • Investigated the formation of Large Low-Shear-Velocity Provinces (LLSVPs) and mantle plumes.

  • Graduate Research Assistant, UIUC, 2017 – 2022
    • Developed global and regional mantle convection models with data assimilation.

  • Graduate Research Assistant, USTC, 2014 – 2017
    • Studied the uplift of the Tibetan Plateau and its steep margins.
    • Analyzed crustal channel flow using analytical and numerical methods.
  • Undergraduate Research Assistant, USTC, 2013 – 2014
    • Explored the evolution of the Tibetan Plateau with numerical models.
    • Studied the thermal structure of the continental lithosphere from heat-flow data.

Research Interests

  • Developing and applying novel numerical-modeling techniques.
  • The interplay between mantle dynamics and tectonic plates.
  • The diverse range of subduction processes and their mechanisms.
  • The formation, evolution, and dynamics of Large Low-Shear-Velocity Provinces and mantle plumes.
  • Plume–slab interactions and the resulting lithospheric deformation and volcanism.
  • Using geophysical, geochemical, and geological data to constrain mantle dynamics and lithospheric structure.
